HK Tramways

an enhanced mobile experience

CHALLENGE

In recent years, HK Tramways has experienced continual patronage loss due to challenging and competitive operating environments.

BUSINESS GOAL

Improve HK Tramway's digital offerings to lead to quick-win improvements to increase ridership during off-peak hours

OUTCOME

  • Introduced onboarding assistance for new users to improve learnability of service
  • Simplified existing user flows to improve usability of existing features
  • Streamlined UI to become consistent with brand identiy

PROJECT DETAILS

duration: 2 weeks
team: Anthony Fan, Denise Ding

This was a General Assembly class project and is not affiliated with HK Tramways

TACTICS

Comparative Analysis, Heuristic Evaluation, Contextual Inquiry, Interviews, Surveys, Personas, Journey Mapping, Rapid Prototyping, Usability Testing

TOOLS

OmniGraffle, Justinmind, GoogleForms, Kanban Board, Keynote

DETAILED CASE STUDY:

BACKGROUND

For over a century, HK Tramways has been a significant mode of transportation traversing the most heavily populated stretch of HK Island.

PHASE 1: DISCOVERY

Business Analysis

To start off with, we primed ourselves with information on HKTâ€™s business model and current digital offerings to understand it's strengths/weaknesses

I spent a lot of time sifting through affiliated websites and social media outlets, which unexpectedly revealed to me that the tram is not only popular with tourists, but offers sentimental value for many HK natives. I was curious as to how to introduce those sentiments to riders who had no knowledge of the tram

We had an inkling that we wanted to focus on mobile digital tools such as it's app or kiosks/displays - tools that can be accessed immediately to aid tip planning. So, we swiftly evaluated HKT's current mobile app:

Current issues:

  • poor task orientation to support user goals (features embedded are not easily accessible)
  • cluttered aesthetics and UI that appears drastically different from it's website's visuals
  • does not support mobile ergonomics

User Research

With some background knowledge, the next step was to go for a ride! We needed to get our own feel for the environment during off-peak hours, and the contexts of use for passengers.

We set to to understand the following:

  • Who's uses the tram and what are their most common scenarios of travel
  • What turns people off from the tram experience?
  • What do current tram riders enjoy about their tram experience?
  • What are the barriers toward existing tram digital tools?
  • What are the specific points of confusion during a rider's journey?

After conducting informal interviews on and off the tram with riders and potential riders, we formed some hypothesis that we then sought to validated by sending out an online survey through Google Forms.

Results from over 60 people lead us to conclude that:

  • unclear navigation information and usage directions is a major hindrance for new or infrequent riders
    • the 3rd most common negative experience rider has had (after crowdedness and physical discomforts)
  • the tram becomes is a pleasurable method of travel when one has more leisure time
    • 2/3 survey participants feel that the tram is a relaxing way to travel and can provide a valuable cultural experience
  • unpredictable travel times turn potential passengers away to other modes of transportation
    • 68% are willing to wait for the tram for less than 5mins before option for another form of transport. However, from our observation - tram often takes more than 5 mins to arrive

Key insights

  • users with high potential are passengers using the tram for leisure/recreational needs
  • the existing digital tools offered do not adequately address user needs
  • The tram is a cultural emblem that differs from typical public transports.

PHASE 2: DEFINE

Data Synthesis

Next, we analyzed our data employing several methods. I found most meaningful way for the data to form into themes was to categorize user behaviors to form into personas, or archetypes to represent HKT's rider base. From there, the rest of the decisions made could all be guided by how the design/feature could impact our personas.

In the interest of limiting the scope of the project, we chose to focus on riders who can benefit from digital tools, specifically for mobile devices.

Another helpful way for the team to communicate our ideas to one another about how we interpreted the contextual inquiry findings was to storyboard scenarios that our personas encounter. These quick sketches will eventually inform the user flow of the digital product that we aim to build.

Feature Prioritisation

Vivi, Jacques, and Blondie had vastly different motivations for riding. We identified some of the most urgent needs they had and treated these painpoints as design opportunities that translated to possible features.

We empathized with our rider's needs and frustrations - but needed to clarify which problems we can help HKT solve given current time and budget constraints.

Seeing what features HKT's direct mobile app competitors (indicated by survey results) had gave us a clue as to how we can meet riders' expectations.

findings: potential differentiaor in a "ride guide"
HKT's lagging behind on: point-to-point seseach, favourite/bookmark

Plotting the possible features helped keep us on track in terms of what can realistically accomplished in the current iteration of the app, and helped us finalise the decision to redesign the existing app rather than create another app and incurring additional marketing expenses for HKT to promote the app

Strategy

Help HK Tramways meet consumer expectations of how a transport mobile app behaviors by catering to the rider's goals.

  • introduced onboarding assistance for tourists to improve learnability of service
  • updated IA and UI to improve overall usability
  • simplify existing (nexTram and nearby stops) features for daily commuters

PHASE 3: DESIGN

User Flow

I wanted to understand the structure of information on the existing app. There are elements (such as very detailed catering services abroad party trams for hire) that I believe riders don't need to access via the app. It would be critial at this point to receive client approval on the content strategy in the redesign.

The user flow for the existing app provided some ideas on where extra steps can be elimited

The user flow evolved into low-fidelity wireframes, which are fast to make and edit on Omnigraffle (as opposed to more detailed wireframes). Chose to follow the scenario of Vivi the Tourist becasuse her flow covers the most states in the app.

Prototyping

From there, I explored different interactions and navigation patterns through sketching

Iterating and testing the interactions as we sketched was a great to generate, explore, and ultimiately scrap many ideas in the most time and cost effective way.

Usability Testing

The prototyping software Justinmind was a quick way of jumping directly from paper to interactive prototype, skipping the visual mockup stage usually done in Sketch3.

With an interactive prototype, we tested with 3 users recruited from survey participants.

CONCLUSION

What's next?

Usability test in the field

When conducting usability testing, it's one thing to sit in a room and be walked through a scenario, but to try to get tram riders to use the prototype on the go would for sure yield unexpected results.

Identify and consider further use cases

HKT riders range widely. During the 2-week project, we were only able to focus on three types of behavioral themes. It would be interesting to conduct further research to examine additional opportunities in edge cases.

Design and test microinteractions to comply with platform interface guidelines

Once the app is approved for relaunch, I'd move on to refining the more nuanced interactions within the user flow. For example, what feedback would best assist a user when there is no next tram available due to temporary construction of tracks?
